It matters where you put things, because of the comfort in the users hands.

But what about tablets?

Unlike the phone, tablets don't have a conventional grip

In general the user will grip around the middle of the screen

Important implications

The bottom of the screen on a tablet is not a touch-friendly place

An elegant visual design can fail for ergonomic reasons

Favor sides and corners & avoid top center

Gestures are the keyboard shortcuts of touch

  • tap
  • double tap
  • tap and hold
  • swipe
  • pinch
  • spread

Present data objects as physical objects

Acts like is more important than looks like

The browser restricts us to two gestures

  • tap
  • swipe

This is because the browser has its own gestures

Gesture conventions

  • Tap: activate, show me more
  • Swipe: scroll, next/previous, flipping cards, reveal hidden panel
  • Double tap: zoom in and out
  • Pinch & Spread: zoom in and out
  • Tap and hold: reveal contextual menu

How do you find out about gestures?

  • Rely on visual cues or past experience
  • The less a gesture resembles a physical action, the more difficult it will be to find
